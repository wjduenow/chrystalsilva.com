More about me, and my life.

I am originally from Sri Lanka, I arrived here in December 1999, starting the New Year in New York city. The Bay Area was where my mom and my sister moved settled. My father was still living in Sri Lanka because he was a Colonel at the Sri Lankan army fighting a civil war for what seemed like an eternity. I would visit my father during my summer holidays and I could not believe how different the two worlds I was living in were. I was living in the Bay Area with everything I could ask for and more. My father finally re-joined our family after five almost six years apart and the war was finished two years later in 2008.

Going back to Sri Lanka every few years, I have seen my birth country growing and changing. My father has always encouraged us to be sustainable, recycle and not to waste (even food). This last semester in school has really helped me focus and take action on something that I have been passionate about, and that is finding solutions to the waste problems we face in our community day to day.

ARTH 15 - Visual Culture

Introduction to Visual Culture examines the visual culture that pervades everyday life in our increasingly complex image-saturated age. From painting to advertisements, from the Internet to posters, we come across visual culture in a variety of ways and forms. This course will take up key ideas and debates pertaining to visual culture through an interdisciplinary study that critically considers art and media as significant social forces. The course will discuss the role visual culture plays in contemporary life and will provide methods with which to read images, understand cultural context, and assess changes in spectatorship and distribution that have ensued due to new forms of image production and dissemination. Course Goals After taking this course students will gain a critical understanding of how visual culture informs everyday life and experience. Readings, lectures and discussions will introduce theoretical and historical approaches to the study of visual culture while examining key issues in the study of media, art history, and culture at large. Students will learn techniques for analyzing images and objects encountered in art and culture.

Assignment- To pick an image, and describe what you see. Discussing how the image reflects the ideas which we have been exploring in the semester such as the role of the viewer in creating meaning, as well as the importance of power relationships, and the change of those relationships over time.
